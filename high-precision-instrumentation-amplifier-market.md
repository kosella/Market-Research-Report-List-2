<p><p>High precision instrumentation amplifiers are classified into two main types based on frequency range - low frequency and high frequency. Low frequency instrumentation amplifiers are designed to accurately measure and amplify signals with frequencies below a certain threshold, typically in the range of a few Hz to a few kHz. On the other hand, high frequency instrumentation amplifiers are specifically tailored for signals with frequencies above the low frequency range, typically in the kHz to GHz range. Each type of instrumentation amplifier is optimized for its specific frequency range to ensure high precision and accuracy in signal amplification.</p></p>
