<p><p>Automotive Integrated Circuits (ICs) are classified into two main types: Monolithic Integrated Circuits and Hybrid Integrated Circuits. Monolithic ICs are constructed on a single semiconductor crystal, offering high reliability and low cost. On the other hand, Hybrid ICs combine the advantages of both thick film and thin film technologies, providing high performance and versatility. The Monolithic ICs market focuses on cost-effective and reliable solutions, while the Hybrid ICs market emphasizes high performance and flexibility for automotive applications.</p></p>
