<p><p>External hard drives come in two main types: HDD and SSD. HDD (Hard Disk Drive) external drives use spinning disks to store data, offering high capacity at a lower cost. SSD (Solid State Drive) external drives store data on flash memory chips, providing faster speeds and improved durability. The HDD market is popular for users needing large amounts of storage at an affordable price, while the SSD market caters to those looking for faster data transfer speeds and a more reliable storage solution.</p></p>
