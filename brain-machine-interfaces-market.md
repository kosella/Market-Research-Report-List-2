<p><p>Brain Machine Interfaces (BMI) market is divided into two main types: Invasive and Non-Invasive. Invasive BMIs involve implanting electrodes directly into the brain tissue, providing higher precision and control but requiring surgery and posing higher risks. Non-Invasive BMIs, on the other hand, use external devices like EEG caps to monitor brain activity without the need for surgery, offering safer and less intrusive options for users. Both types of BMIs have unique advantages and applications in various fields such as healthcare, gaming, and communication.</p></p>
