<p><p>Automotive engine connecting rods are crucial components that transfer motion from the piston to the crankshaft. There are three main types of materials used for connecting rods: aluminium, steel, and titanium. Aluminium rods are lightweight and provide good strength-to-weight ratio. Steel rods offer high durability and are commonly used in high-performance engines. Titanium rods are the most expensive but offer exceptional strength and lightweight properties, making them ideal for high-performance racing applications. Each material type has its own set of benefits and is chosen based on the specific needs of the engine.</p></p>
