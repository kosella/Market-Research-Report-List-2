<p><p>The aircraft turbojet engine market can be divided into two main types: axial-flow type turbojets and centrifugal type turbojets. Axial-flow type turbojets have a design where air flows parallel to the axis of the engine, allowing for efficient and high-speed performance. On the other hand, centrifugal type turbojets use a design where air is pushed outward by centrifugal force, allowing for simpler construction and lower manufacturing costs. Both types of turbojets are commonly used in a variety of aircraft applications, ranging from military fighter jets to commercial airliners.</p></p>
