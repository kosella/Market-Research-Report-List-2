<p><ul><li>Sintered</li><li>Bonded</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Ferrite arc magnets are commonly classified into two main types: sintered and bonded. Sintered ferrite arc magnets are produced through a process of compacting fine ferrite powder in a die and then sintering it at high temperatures to achieve the desired shape and properties. On the other hand, bonded ferrite arc magnets are made by mixing ferrite powder with a binder material and then pressing it into the desired shape. These two types of ferrite arc magnets cater to different application requirements and offer varying performance characteristics.</p></p>
