<p><p>Natural asphalt can be classified based on its sources, such as derived from coal and derived from oil. Asphalt derived from coal is formed through the natural distillation of bituminous coal, resulting in a dense and durable material. On the other hand, asphalt derived from oil is a byproduct of refining crude oil, producing a more versatile and widely-used material. Both types of natural asphalt have unique properties and applications in construction, road paving, and waterproofing projects.</p></p>
