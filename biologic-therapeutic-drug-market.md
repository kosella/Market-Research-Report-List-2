<p><p>Biologic therapeutic drugs are categorized into three main types: Monoclonal antibodies, therapeutic proteins, and vaccines. Monoclonal antibodies are engineered proteins that target specific cells or proteins in the body to treat diseases. Therapeutic proteins are proteins that are used to replace or enhance the function of naturally occurring proteins in the body. Vaccines are substances that stimulate the immune system to produce immunity against specific diseases. These types of biologic drugs play a crucial role in treating a wide range of health conditions and preventing infectious diseases.</p></p>
