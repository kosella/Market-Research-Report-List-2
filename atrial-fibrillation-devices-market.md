Catheter Ablation involves using a catheter to destroy tissue in the heart that is causing irregular electrical signals, while Maze Surgery is a more invasive procedure that creates scar tissue to redirect electrical signals in the heart. Both procedures aim to restore normal rhythm and reduce symptoms of Atrial Fibrillation.</p></p>
