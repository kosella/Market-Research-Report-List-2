<p><p>The automotive bumper market is typically divided into two main segments: front bumper and rear bumper. Front bumpers are designed to absorb impact during front-end collisions and protect vital components like the engine and radiator. Rear bumpers, on the other hand, are meant to prevent damage to the vehicle's trunk and rear components in the event of a rear-end collision. Both types of bumpers play a crucial role in ensuring the safety and structural integrity of a vehicle.</p></p>
